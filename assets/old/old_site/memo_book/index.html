About Me

Who Am I?A Short Bio

I was born in the Philippines in 1993, and spent the first 3 years of my childhood in my homeland. My parents moved the family to America in 1996 when they landed jobs as Information Technology professionals. I was exposed early on the various tasks and functions that my parents performed at their respective jobs, and that heavily influenced my decision and interest in the area of Information Technology. I took my first programming class using Visual Basic during my sophomore year of high school, and it was such an affirming and rewarding experience when I developed my first GUI screen and saw it perform its intended function right before my eyes. It felt like building my first robot; bringing life to an inanimate object. I pursued additional programming classes in the following years. I was introduced to Java in my junior year, and then HTML, CSS, and PHP in my senior year. I knew then that when I leave high school, I would like to focus and venture towards acquiring an education, knowledge and honing my skills that would prepare me for a career in Information Technology.


At the present time, while most of my skills and experience are focused towards web development, I have also dabbled in application development and engineering. I aim to continuously learn and implement applications in new programming languages, and keep myself up to date with new technologies and development platforms. From Java to Node.js to jQuery, I am interested in learning them all.


Lastly, I am also interested in focusing on the information systems field, learning about various facets of the Software Development Life Cycle, and building applications for business processes and business functions. It is my ultimate goal to obtain experience and exposure on various roles within SDLC not only in programming, debugging and defect resolution, but also in areas of Quality Assurance, Systems Analysis, Data Analysis... the possibilities are endless!
